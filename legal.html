New York BitLicense — deep dive
▾ expand
For individuals

No license required to hold, mine, or use Monero personally. BitLicense applies only to businesses.

For businesses

Must apply for BitLicense ($5,000 fee, extensive compliance). Most exchanges block NY. Only ~30 licenses issued since 2015.

For mining

Personal mining legal. Commercial may need permits. RandomX (CPU) not specifically targeted by PoW moratorium.

Privacy coins

No NY law specifically bans privacy coins. NYDFS informally discourages listings.

Bottom line

In NY you can legally hold, mine, and send Monero. Buy via P2P, atomic swaps, or non-custodial exchanges (Haveno, Trocador).

Monero is legal in the vast majority of tracked jurisdictions. The trend is toward regulation, not prohibition. Privacy is not illegal.
