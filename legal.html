New York BitLicense — deep dive expand ▾

New York's BitLicense (23 NYCRR Part 200) is the most restrictive cryptocurrency regulation in the United States. It requires any business engaging in virtual currency activity with NY residents to obtain a license from the NY Department of Financial Services (NYDFS).

For individuals: No license required to hold, mine, or use Monero personally. BitLicense applies only to businesses. You can hold XMR in NY legally.
For businesses: Must apply for BitLicense ($5,000 application fee, extensive compliance program). Most exchanges don't bother — they simply block NY users. Only ~30 BitLicenses have been issued since 2015.
For mining: Personal mining is legal in NY. Commercial mining operations may need permits based on electricity usage (see Moratorium on Proof-of-Work Mining Act, which applied to carbon-based PoW — Monero's RandomX is CPU-based and not specifically targeted).
Privacy coins specifically: No NY law specifically bans privacy coins. However, most licensed exchanges in NY exclude Monero because the NYDFS informally discourages listings of privacy-enhanced cryptocurrencies.
Bottom line: In NY, you can legally hold, mine, and send Monero. You cannot easily buy it through exchanges that serve NY. Use P2P, atomic swaps, or non-custodial exchanges without NY restrictions (Haveno, Trocador).
