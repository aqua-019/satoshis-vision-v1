Privacy Technology

Monero's Three Privacy Layers

Each layer addresses a specific privacy leak that exists in Bitcoin.

Ring Signatures

Sender Privacy — Who sent it?

Ring signatures mix the sender's transaction with decoy outputs from the blockchain, making it mathematically impossible to determine which input is the real one. This is exactly what Satoshi described as "group signatures" in 2010.

Bitcoin

Every input is directly traceable. Chain analysis companies like Chainalysis can follow funds from address to address with high confidence.

Monero

Ring size of 16 means each transaction is mixed with 15 decoys. An observer cannot determine which input is real.

Stealth Addresses

Receiver Privacy — Who received it?

One-time addresses are generated for each transaction so the receiver's actual address never appears on the blockchain. Satoshi described this as "blinded variations of a public key."

Bitcoin

Addresses are reused or linkable. Once an address is tied to an identity, all its transactions are exposed.

Monero

A unique one-time address is created for every transaction. Only the recipient can identify and spend the funds.

RingCT (Confidential Transactions)

Amount Privacy — How much was sent?

Pedersen commitments and range proofs hide transaction amounts while still allowing the network to verify that no coins were created from nothing. Satoshi's workaround was "standard denominations" — RingCT solved it cryptographically.

Bitcoin

Every transaction amount is fully visible. Anyone can see exactly how much BTC moved and calculate wallet balances.

Monero

Amounts are hidden using Pedersen commitments. The network verifies conservation of value without revealing values.

Philosophy

Two Visions for Digital Money

Bitcoin chose to become digital gold — a transparent, institutional asset that trades in ETFs and sits on corporate balance sheets. Monero chose to become digital cash — private, fungible, and resistant to surveillance.

The fundamental philosophical divergence

Bitcoin's Path

Bitcoin has been embraced by traditional finance. ETFs, institutional custody, government reserves, and corporate treasuries. Its transparent ledger makes it compatible with regulatory frameworks. Some argue this was always the path.

Monero's Path

Monero has been delisted from 73+ exchanges, targeted by government bounties, and declared unbreakable. It thrives on peer-to-peer trading and decentralized infrastructure. Satoshi's original cypherpunk vision, realized.
