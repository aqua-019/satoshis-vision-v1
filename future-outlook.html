Section 01 — The Regulatory Landscape

Legislation Timeline: 2024–2027

A new legal architecture is being built around cryptocurrency. Some of it grants genuine freedoms. All of it builds surveillance infrastructure.

May 2024
FIT21 — Financial Innovation and Technology for the 21st Century Act Pro-Crypto

Passed the House 279–136. Establishes the CFTC as the primary crypto regulator. Creates three classifications: digital commodities, restricted digital assets, and payment stablecoins. The most significant pro-crypto legislation in U.S. history — but every classification comes with a reporting obligation.

January 2025
GENIUS Act Stablecoin

Federal licensing framework for stablecoin issuers. Requires 1:1 reserves, monthly attestations, and full AML/BSA compliance. Passed the Senate Banking Committee in March 2025. Establishes stablecoins as the regulated on-ramp — and every on-ramp is a surveillance checkpoint.

February 2025
CLARITY Act Pro-Crypto

Exempts certain digital assets from securities classification if sufficiently decentralized. Provides safe harbor protections for developers. Genuine freedom — but "decentralized" is defined by the regulators, and the exemption requires ongoing compliance reporting.

March 2025
Executive Order: Strategic Bitcoin Reserve Government

~200,000 BTC held by the U.S. government from seizures and forfeitures. Permanent institutional adoption and permanent on-chain government surveillance capability. The government now has a direct financial interest in Bitcoin's traceability.

2025–2026
IRS Digital Asset Reporting Rules Surveillance

Exchanges and DeFi front-ends must report all transactions. Cost basis, capital gains/losses, and wallet addresses are tracked and reported to the IRS. Effective for the 2025 tax year. Form 1099-DA creates a permanent paper trail for every on-chain movement.

2025–2027
Travel Rule Expansion (FATF) Surveillance

Sender and receiver identity must be transmitted for all transfers above $1,000. Global enforcement via FATF member nations. Turns every compliant exchange into a financial surveillance relay. Combined with chain analysis, this creates a complete graph of Bitcoin ownership.

Section 04 — The ZCash Fallacy

Privacy Theater vs. Privacy by Default

ZCash is often cited as a Monero alternative. The data tells a different story.

ZCash was created by the Electric Coin Company (ECC), led by CEO Zooko Wilcox. It was VC-funded from inception — investors include Pantera Capital, Digital Currency Group (Barry Silbert), Naval Ravikant, and Roger Ver. This is not a community project. This is a venture-backed company with a product.

The Founders' Reward: For the first four years, 20% of all ZCash mining rewards went directly to founders, investors, and the ECC. After the initial reward expired, the "Dev Fund" continued the 20% allocation. Miners and users fund the company that controls the protocol.

The trusted setup problem: ZCash's zk-SNARKs required a trusted setup ceremony. If any participant in that ceremony retained their secret parameters, they could mint unlimited ZCash undetectably. This is unverifiable — you must trust that everyone involved destroyed their keys. Monero has no trusted setup. Its privacy is mathematical, not ceremonial.
